**Tomb Worlds** are the worlds which have been inhabited by dormant Necrons for millions of years.

## Overview

Their worlds exist in unknown numbers throughout many parts of the galaxy. A Tomb World is seemingly long abandoned, and many are thought to be the ancient worlds of the Necrontyr, since their architecture shows up on many of the planets. Tomb worlds have a tendency to be barren and lifeless desert worlds.

Many Adeptus Mechanicus personnel sent to these planets for exploration are often never heard from again; the vile Necron inhabitants never having truly left over the millions of years they had been in stasis.

In absence of an active Necron population, most Tomb Worlds are managed by a master control program dubbed a Deep Spirit who oversees its slumbering denizens. This program oversees the Tomb Worlds swarms of Canoptek Constructs which serve as laborers, caretakers, and defenders. While some Tomb Worlds are obviously alien in nature and home to great Necron cities, others are hidden and their structures and inhabitants may be entombed deep beneath the surface or even isolated in pocket dimensions that prevent their discovery by subsequent civilizations. The hidden nature of Tomb Worlds was in part enacted to protect them from marauding Eldar sixty million years ago. Only a tiny portion of Tomb Worlds have since been discovered or reawakened, and the number is far greater than any save the Necrons themselves realize.

### Types of Tomb Worlds

* **Crownworlds**: Were once the hubs of galactic Necrontyr power. Necrons from crownworlds tend to have survived the great sleep in excellent condition and the worlds are untouched by disaster or other races. As a result, these tend to be the hubs of Necron Dynasties and the Necron Overlords rule from them.
* **Coreworlds**: Are next in importance after crownworlds. These planets form the heart of a Necron Dynasty.
* **Fringeworlds**: Are planets of tertiary importance, not viewed as being high enough in status to be numbered amongst a dynasty's coreworlds. Fringeworlds tend to be poor and distant colonies, used for manual labor.
